Digital principles
BritishRedCross

Help people in crisis

Do things based on the needs of our users. Not what we want or think they need.

People who use our services in a crisis. Front line staff and volunteers who help them. People who support the charity.

It's why we’re here.

Prove there's a need

Identify user needs and have evidence they exist first.

Do research. Talk to users. Watch what they do. They're not the same thing.

Analyse data. Use analytics. See what users search for.

Set the benchmark

Plan how to meet user needs and measure if they’re met. If you can’t measure it, don’t do it.

Pick common and useful metrics. Set benchmarks. Continue to get feedback from users. Improve things in the future.

Go live is the beginning not the end.

Start small and iterate

Build quick prototypes. Test with users early and often.

Go live with just enough. Get feedback. Add things. Take them away.

Don’t do ‘big bangs’ and overwhelm users.

Reduce risk. Work fast. Fail fast. Learn from it.

Decide with data

If you have to make assumptions. If you need to make a decision. Test with users. Look at analytics.

Don’t spend ages working on something without getting feedback from users. Go to where they are.

Design with data and research. Not committee.

If it works use it

Use things that already help meet a need. Don’t reinvent the wheel.

Use design patterns. Follow conventions. Use APIs. Update content that exists. Make things better.

Be consistent. Users only learn things once. They trust us.

Avoid duplication. Save time. Focus energy and creativity on where it’s needed. Solve new problems.

If it doesn't work change it

Do things based on evidence. Not habit.

Don't keep working on something that no one uses. Scrap it if isn’t working.

Don’t be afraid to change how you work. Our users’ needs might change, so should we.

Don't do it alone

Share what you do and involve people around you.

They spot things you miss. They bring new ideas.

It best helps meet the needs of our users. It make things better.

Make it easy

Make things simple and accessible.

Create things that benefit people. Help them do what they care about.

Understand context. Don't throw technology at the problem.

Help them be independent. Use data to improve what they do.
